About me

I am a research scientist and project team leader Ksilink, a biotechnology company in Strasbourg, France. Together with a team of biologists, lab automation specialists, and data scientists we create and explore stem cell-based neuronal disease models using high-content imaging. Previously, I worked as a postdoc at Sanofi with Barbara Calamini on Huntington's disease. I did my PhD work in Jeffrey Chao's lab at the Friedrich Miescher Institute for Biomedical Research on mRNA translation imaging in living cells.

Research interests

I am interested in interdisciplinary research combining biology, microscopic imaging, and computational data analysis to address biomedical challenges. I have built up expertise in imaging human cellular biology in living and fixed cells, imaging-based drug screening, and image segmentation, particle tracking and colocalization analysis.
Currently I am focusing on the application of machine learning for cellular characterization based on imaging data.
